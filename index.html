Welcome To Georgia

Georgia, or as the local call it, Sakartvelo, nestled at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, captivates travelers with its rich history, stunning landscapes, and warm hospitality. This enchanting country boasts a tapestry of ancient churches, fortresses perched on rugged hillsides, and vibrant cities steeped in culture. Tbilisi, its bustling capital, offers a blend of modern architecture alongside cobblestone streets lined with cozy cafes.For nature enthusiasts, the Caucasus Mountains provide breathtaking scenery and opportunities for hiking and skiing.

Georgia's culinary scene is equally enticing, with savory khachapuri and aromatic wines that trace their roots back thousands of years. The country's unique alphabet, one of the world's oldest, adds to its allure.

Whether exploring the UNESCO-listed sites, soaking in the therapeutic waters of Borjomi, or savoring the charm of its traditional polyphonic music, Georgia promises an unforgettable journey through a land where tradition meets innovation.

Fun Facts About Sakartvelo

This gorgeous country has its own surprising facts and here only few of them:

Georgia is one of the oldest wine producing regions in the world, and it is believed to be the first country in the world where wine is made
The most famous native of Georgia is Joseph Stalin, the second ruler of the Soviet Union and one of the greatest dictators of the twentieth century
Tbilisi produces one of the best brandy drinks in the world. The Sarajishvili Brewery gained worldwide recognition during the Second World War, when Churchill tasted the brandy served to him by Stalin and claimed it was the best drink that ever came to his lips
Archaeologists recently discovered a 1.8-million-year-old skull in Damanisi, near the Caucasus Mountains. This find is a key to an ancient connection of migrations between Africa and Europe.
The mineral waters of Georgia are famous all over the world. Borjomi is a resort and healing town located in south-central Georgia, and has about twenty springs of mineral water known for their healing properties.
Georgia is the third country in the world to accept Christianity, in 328 AD.
